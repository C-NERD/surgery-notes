
- # Hypertrophic scar
	- ![Hypertrophic scar — single linear raised scar confined within original wound boundary](../assets/scar-hypertrophic-clinical-neck.jpg)
	- *Clinical example — single linear raised scar confined within the original wound margin. Source: hist Wound Healing & Repair (UDU Sokoto) slide 23.*
	- **Definition** — a raised ([[Elevated scars|elevated]]) scar resulting from excessive fibroblastic activity during wound healing in which the **excess collagen is laid down in a parallel pattern**, the scar **does not extend beyond the boundary of the original wound**, and it **eventually regresses** spontaneously.
		- Source: Bailey & Love Ch. 3 p. 49 — *"hypertrophic scars do not extend beyond the boundary of the original incision or wound and eventually regress"*; collagen pattern *"arranged in a... parallel pattern in hypertrophic scars"*.
		- Source: Baja Ch. 5 p. 68 — mechanism: *"excessive fibroblastic activity with marked granulation tissue formation resulting in a markedly raised scar"*.
		- Source: hist Wound Healing slide 23 — *"Elevated scars: Hypertrophic scars — if contained within sight of original wound"*.
	- Contrast with [[Keloid scar]] (extends beyond margin, does not regress). For the comparison table, see [[Elevated scars]].
