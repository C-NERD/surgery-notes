- # Systemic Inflammatory Response Syndrome
	- **A clinical syndrome of generalised inflammatory activation, defined by ≥ 2 of the following criteria** (ACCP/SCCM consensus, 1991):
		- Temperature > 38 °C or < 36 °C (rectal)
		- Heart rate > 90 / min
		- Respiratory rate > 20 / min, or PaCO₂ < 32 mmHg
		- WBC > 12 × 10⁹/L, or < 4 × 10⁹/L, or > 10% immature (band) forms
		- Source: Baja Ch. 8 p. 100
	- **Causes** — both infectious and non-infectious:
		- *Infectious*: bacteria, viruses, fungi, protozoa
		- *Non-infectious*: acute pancreatitis, burns, trauma, hypovolaemic shock, autoimmune disease, drug fever
		- Source: Baja Ch. 8 p. 100
	- **Relationships**:
		- SIRS + infection → [[Sepsis]]
		- SIRS itself is non-specific — many ill patients meet criteria without sepsis, and a 2015 ANZ study found ~12% of severe-sepsis patients were SIRS-negative.
		- Source: Baja Ch. 8 p. 100
