
- # Keloid scar
	- ![Multiple keloid scars — raised lesions across chest and arm extending beyond original wound margins](../assets/keloid-scars-multiple-bl-fig-3-5.jpg)
	- *Multiple keloid scars on the chest and arm. Source: Bailey & Love Ch. 3 p. 49, Fig. 3.5.*
	- **Definition** — a raised ([[Elevated scars|elevated]]) scar resulting from excessive fibroblastic activity during wound healing in which the **excess collagen is laid down in a disorganised pattern**, the scar **extends beyond the boundary of the original wound**, and it **does not spontaneously regress**.
		- Source: Bailey & Love Ch. 3 p. 49 — *"keloid scars extend beyond the boundaries of the original incision or wound… do not spontaneously regress and are difficult to treat"*; collagen pattern *"arranged in a disorganised pattern in keloid scars"*.
		- Source: Baja Ch. 5 p. 68 — predilection: *"the ear, beard area, neck and chest areas are frequent sites for such lesions"*; predisposed in *"dark skinned people"*; *"particularly liable to occur in the scars of burns"*.
		- Source: hist Wound Healing slide 23 — *"Elevated scars: Keloid scars — if extended beyond original wound"*.
	- Contrast with [[Hypertrophic scar]] (stays within margin, regresses). For the comparison table, see [[Elevated scars]].
